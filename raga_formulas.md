# Indian Raga Scale Formulas - 20 Most Common Ragas

## Notation Convention

**Number Notation (1-7):**
- **1** = Sa (Tonic)
- **2** = Re (Second)
- **3** = Ga (Third)
- **4** = Ma (Fourth)
- **5** = Pa (Fifth)
- **6** = Dha (Sixth)
- **7** = Ni (Seventh)

**Accidentals:**
- **b** = komal (flat)
- **#** = tivra (sharp) - only applies to Ma (4)
- **bb** = ati-komal (double-flat) - very rare
- **##** = double-sharp (not used in Indian ragas)
- **b/** = half-flat (quarter-tone) - not standard in these ragas

**Sargam Notation Convention:**
- Uppercase = shuddha (natural): S, R, G, P, D, N
- Lowercase = komal (flat): r, g, d, n
- **Exception for Ma**: lowercase 'm' = shuddha Ma, uppercase 'M' = tivra Ma (sharp)

## The 10 Thaats (Parent Scales)

These are the 10 foundational scales from which all ragas derive:

1. **Bilawal**: `1 2 3 4 5 6 7` | S R G m P D N | (Ionian/Major)
2. **Kalyan**: `1 2 3 4# 5 6 7` | S R G M P D N | (Lydian)
3. **Khamaj**: `1 2 3 4 5 6 7b` | S R G m P D n | (Mixolydian)
4. **Bhairav**: `1 2b 3 4 5 6b 7` | S r G m P d N | (Double Harmonic)
5. **Poorvi**: `1 2b 3 4# 5 6b 7` | S r G M P d N | (Double Harmonic #4)
6. **Marwa**: `1 2b 3 4# 5 6 7` | S r G M P D N | (Lydian b2)
7. **Kafi**: `1 2 3b 4 5 6 7b` | S R g m P D n | (Dorian)
8. **Asavari**: `1 2 3b 4 5 6b 7b` | S R g m P d n | (Aeolian/Natural Minor)
9. **Bhairavi**: `1 2b 3b 4 5 6b 7b` | S r g m P d n | (Phrygian)
10. **Todi**: `1 2b 3b 4# 5 6b 7` | S r g M P d N | (Phrygian #4 #7)

## 20 Most Common Ragas - Complete Formulas

### 1. Yaman (Kalyan)
- **Formula**: `1 2 3 4# 5 6 7`
- **Swaras**: S R G M P D N S
- **Thaat**: Kalyan
- **Notes**: All shuddha notes except tivra Ma (sharp 4th). Equivalent to Lydian mode. Very popular, sung in evening.
- **Jati**: Sampurna-Sampurna (7 notes ascending and descending)

### 2. Bhupali (Bhup)
- **Formula**: `1 2 3 5 6` (pentatonic - omits 4 and 7)
- **Swaras**: S R G P D S
- **Thaat**: Kalyan
- **Notes**: Pentatonic (5 notes), all natural/shuddha. Equivalent to major pentatonic. Very common beginner raga.
- **Jati**: Audava-Audava (5 notes ascending and descending)

### 3. Todi (Miyan ki Todi)
- **Formula**: `1 2b 3b 4# 5 6b 7`
- **Swaras**: S r g M P d N S
- **Thaat**: Todi
- **Notes**: Uses komal Re, komal Ga (ati-komal, especially flat), tivra Ma, komal Dha, shuddha Ni. Morning raga with serious mood.
- **Jati**: Sampurna-Sampurna

### 4. Kafi
- **Formula**: `1 2 3b 4 5 6 7b`
- **Swaras**: S R g m P D n S
- **Thaat**: Kafi
- **Notes**: Uses komal Ga and komal Ni. Equivalent to Dorian mode. Folk-influenced, light classical.
- **Jati**: Sampurna-Sampurna

### 5. Asavari
- **Formula**: `1 2 3b 4 5 6b 7b`
- **Swaras**: S R g m P d n S
- **Thaat**: Asavari
- **Notes**: Uses komal Ga, komal Dha, komal Ni. Equivalent to Aeolian/Natural Minor. Morning raga, serious mood.
- **Jati**: Sampurna-Sampurna

### 6. Bilawal (Bilahari)
- **Formula**: `1 2 3 4 5 6 7`
- **Swaras**: S R G m P D N S
- **Thaat**: Bilawal
- **Notes**: All natural/shuddha notes. Equivalent to Ionian mode (major scale). Morning raga.
- **Jati**: Sampurna-Sampurna

### 7. Khamaj
- **Formula**: `1 2 3 4 5 6 7b`
- **Swaras**: S R G m P D n S
- **Thaat**: Khamaj
- **Notes**: Uses komal Ni. Equivalent to Mixolydian mode. Light classical, semi-classical style.
- **Jati**: Sampurna-Sampurna

### 8. Bageshri (Bageshree)
- **Formula**: `1 2 3b 4 5 6 7b`
- **Swaras**: S R g m P D n S
- **Thaat**: Kafi
- **Notes**: Derived from Kafi thaat. Uses komal Ga and komal Ni. Devotional, serene mood. Late night raga.
- **Jati**: Audava-Sampurna (5 notes ascending: S R g P D n S, 7 descending)
- **Aroha**: S g m P D n S (pentatonic - omits Re in ascent in some versions)
- **Avaroha**: S n D P m g R S

### 9. Desh (Des)
- **Formula Aroha**: `1 2 4 5 7` (pentatonic ascending - omits 3 and 6)
- **Formula Avaroha**: `1 2 3 4 5 6 7b 7` (heptatonic descending - uses both komal Ni and shuddha Ni)
- **Swaras Aroha**: S R m P N S
- **Swaras Avaroha**: S n D P m G R (G) S
- **Thaat**: Khamaj
- **Notes**: Audava-Sampurna (pentatonic-heptatonic). Uses tivra Ma in ascent, both madhyams possible. Uses both Ni in descent. Late night raga, folk-influenced.
- **Jati**: Audava-Sampurna

### 10. Malkauns
- **Formula**: `1 3b 4 6b 7b` (pentatonic - omits 2 and 5)
- **Swaras**: S g m d n S
- **Thaat**: Bhairavi
- **Notes**: Pentatonic (5 notes), uses komal Ga, shuddha Ma, komal Dha, komal Ni. Omits Re and Pa. Midnight raga, very serious and meditative mood.
- **Jati**: Audava-Audava

### 11. Durga
- **Formula**: `1 2 4 5 6` (pentatonic - omits 3 and 7)
- **Swaras**: S R m P D S
- **Thaat**: Bilawal
- **Notes**: Pentatonic (5 notes), all natural except shuddha Ma. Related to Bhupali through modal rotation (murchana).
- **Jati**: Audava-Audava

### 12. Shree (Shri, Puriya Dhanashree)
- **Formula**: `1 2b 3 4# 5 6b 7`
- **Swaras**: S r G M P d N S
- **Thaat**: Purvi
- **Notes**: Uses komal Re, shuddha Ga, tivra Ma, komal Dha, shuddha Ni. Very serious, devotional mood. Evening/night raga.
- **Jati**: Sampurna-Sampurna (though some versions are Audava-Sampurna)
- **Aroha**: S r G M P d N S
- **Avaroha**: S N d P M G r S

### 13. Jaunpuri
- **Formula**: `1 2 3b 4 5 6b 7b`
- **Swaras**: S R g m P d n S
- **Thaat**: Asavari
- **Notes**: Same as Asavari thaat - uses komal Ga, komal Dha, komal Ni. Late morning raga, melancholy undertones.
- **Jati**: Shadava-Sampurna (6 notes ascending, 7 descending in some versions)

### 14. Multani
- **Formula Aroha**: `1 2b 3b 4# 5 6b 7` (omits Re in ascent)
- **Formula Avaroha**: `1 2b 3b 4# 5 6b 7`
- **Swaras**: S r g M P d N S
- **Thaat**: Todi
- **Notes**: Uses komal Re, komal Ga, tivra Ma, komal Dha, shuddha Ni. Related to Todi family. Afternoon raga.
- **Jati**: Shadava-Sampurna (Re omitted in aroha typically)
- **Aroha**: S g M P d N S (vakra - Re omitted)
- **Avaroha**: S N d P M g r S

### 15. Darbari Kanada
- **Formula**: `1 2 3b 4 5 6b 7b`
- **Swaras**: S R g m P d n S
- **Thaat**: Asavari
- **Notes**: Same notes as Asavari but with distinctive slow tempo and use of meend (glides). Uses komal Ga, komal Dha, komal Ni. Midnight raga, very serious, "emperor of ragas."
- **Jati**: Sampurna-Sampurna

### 16. Kedar
- **Formula Aroha**: `1 2 4 4# 5 7` (pentatonic - omits 3 and 6)
- **Formula Avaroha**: `1 2 3b 4 4# 5 6 7` (heptatonic with komal Ga)
- **Swaras Aroha**: S R m M P N S (uses both madhyams)
- **Swaras Avaroha**: S N D P M g R S (adds komal Ga and Dha in descent)
- **Thaat**: Kalyan
- **Notes**: Audava-Shadava. Uses both shuddha Ma and tivra Ma. Komal Ga appears in avaroha. Late evening raga.
- **Jati**: Audava-Shadava (5 notes ascending, 6 descending)

### 17. Bihag
- **Formula**: `1 2 3 4 4# 5 6 7`
- **Swaras**: S R G m M P D N S (uses both madhyams)
- **Thaat**: Bilawal
- **Notes**: Sampurna-Sampurna. Uses both shuddha Ma (in avaroha) and tivra Ma (in aroha). All other notes natural. Evening/night raga, romantic mood.
- **Jati**: Sampurna-Sampurna
- **Aroha**: S R G M P N S (tivra Ma in ascent)
- **Avaroha**: S N D P m G R S (shuddha Ma in descent)

### 18. Bhairavi
- **Formula**: `1 2b 3b 4 5 6b 7b`
- **Swaras**: S r g m P d n S
- **Thaat**: Bhairavi
- **Notes**: Uses 4 komal notes - komal Re, komal Ga, komal Dha, komal Ni. Equivalent to Phrygian mode. Versatile raga, can be sung any time, often for closing concerts.
- **Jati**: Sampurna-Sampurna

### 19. Lalit
- **Formula**: `1 2b 3 4 4# 6b 7` (omits 5)
- **Swaras**: S r G m M d N S (uses both madhyams, omits Pa)
- **Thaat**: Purvi (or considered independent)
- **Notes**: Hexatonic (6 notes). Uses komal Re, shuddha Ga, BOTH shuddha Ma and tivra Ma (rare feature), komal Dha, shuddha Ni. Omits Pa. Early morning raga, very serious.
- **Jati**: Shadava-Shadava (6 notes, omits Pa)

### 20. Miyan ki Todi
- **Formula**: `1 2b 3bb 4# 5 6b 7` (note: 3bb represents ati-komal Ga, extremely flat)
- **Swaras**: S r g̲ M P d N S (g̲ = ati-komal Ga)
- **Thaat**: Todi
- **Notes**: Same as Raga Todi (#3 above). Uses komal Re, ati-komal Ga (lower shruti than normal komal Ga), tivra Ma, komal Dha, shuddha Ni. Morning raga, highly venerated.
- **Jati**: Sampurna-Sampurna

## Additional Western/Jazz Scales

### Jazz Minor (Melodic Minor Ascending)
- **Formula**: `1 2 3b 4 5 6 7`
- **Notes**: Natural minor with raised 6th and 7th degrees (compared to natural minor). Fundamental jazz scale.

### Phrygian Dominant (Freygish, Spanish Phrygian)
- **Formula**: `1 2b 3 4 5 6b 7b`
- **Notes**: Phrygian mode with major 3rd. Common in Klezmer, Flamenco, Middle Eastern music.
- **Related Raga**: Similar structure to parts of Bhairav thaat

## Notes on Scale Degree Conversions

### Komal (Flat) Notes
- **2b** = komal Re = minor 2nd (1 semitone above tonic)
- **3b** = komal Ga = minor 3rd (3 semitones)
- **3bb** = ati-komal Ga = extremely flat 3rd (between 2 and 3 semitones, microtonal)
- **6b** = komal Dha = minor 6th (8 semitones)
- **7b** = komal Ni = minor 7th (10 semitones)

### Tivra (Sharp) Note
- **4#** = tivra Ma = augmented 4th / tritone (6 semitones)

### Natural (Shuddha) Notes
- **1** = Sa = tonic (unison)
- **2** = Re = major 2nd (2 semitones)
- **3** = Ga = major 3rd (4 semitones)
- **4** = Ma = perfect 4th (5 semitones)
- **5** = Pa = perfect 5th (7 semitones) - cannot be altered
- **6** = Dha = major 6th (9 semitones)
- **7** = Ni = major 7th (11 semitones)

### Jati Classification
- **Audava**: 5 notes
- **Shadava**: 6 notes
- **Sampurna**: 7 notes
- **Format**: Aroha-Avaroha (ascending-descending)

### Vakra (Zigzag) Patterns
Some ragas use vakra (non-linear) movements where notes are skipped or approached in specific ways. These patterns are NOT reflected in the basic scale formulas above but are important for authentic raga performance. Examples:
- **Multani**: Often skips Re in ascent (S g M instead of S r g M)
- **Kedar**: Uses characteristic vakra phrases

## Western Mode Equivalents

For reference, here are the Western modes that correspond to some thaats:

1. **Ionian (Major)** = Bilawal: 1 2 3 4 5 6 7
2. **Dorian** = Kafi: 1 2 3b 4 5 6 7b
3. **Phrygian** = Bhairavi: 1 2b 3b 4 5 6b 7b
4. **Lydian** = Kalyan: 1 2 3 4# 5 6 7
5. **Mixolydian** = Khamaj: 1 2 3 4 5 6 7b
6. **Aeolian (Natural Minor)** = Asavari: 1 2 3b 4 5 6b 7b
7. **Locrian** = (no direct thaat equivalent due to diminished 5th)

**Special Modes:**
- **Harmonic Minor**: 1 2 3b 4 5 6b 7 (similar to Kirvani in Carnatic music)
- **Double Harmonic** = Bhairav: 1 2b 3 4 5 6b 7
- **Hungarian Minor** ≈ Todi: 1 2b 3b 4# 5 6b 7

## Usage in This Music Notation Editor

When implementing these ragas in the editor:

1. **Default pitch system**: Number notation (1-7) is used by default
2. **Accidentals**: Use `b` for komal (flat), `#` for tivra Ma only
3. **Font rendering**: NotationFont includes glyphs for all accidentals
4. **Scale selection**: Users can select raga mode which applies appropriate accidentals
5. **Key signature**: Ragas with fixed komal notes can have those marked in key signature
6. **Ornaments**: Meend (glides), kan (grace notes), gamak (oscillations) are separate from scale structure

### Implementation Priority

Start with these 5 most common ragas for initial implementation:
1. **Yaman** - most popular beginner raga
2. **Bhupali** - pentatonic, simple
3. **Bilawal** - all natural notes (like major scale)
4. **Kafi** - Dorian-like, folk influence
5. **Bhairavi** - very common, all komal notes except Ma and Pa
